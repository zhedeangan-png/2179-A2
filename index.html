Mind the Access Gap

How evenly do LRT, MRT, Monorail, and BRT Sunway serve everyday mobility across the Klang Valley?

Where the network reaches

Rail access is spatial. Lines and stations cluster in some corridors more than others.

Key pattern

Central KL forms the densest station cluster.

Most lines converge near the city centre. Outer districts follow longer corridor-style lines.

Why it matters

Access is strongest near the urban core. Peripheral areas rely on fewer rail paths.

How to read

Each dot is a station. Colours show rail lines. Larger clusters indicate stronger station concentration.

Access is not only about having stations nearby

Private transport still dominates trips. Waiting time also changes how accessible each line feels.

BRT Sunway as a local connector

BRT Sunway is a short local corridor. Its value comes from linking Sunway stops to wider rail interchanges.

Final takeaway

Klang Valley rail access is uneven. Station location, line demand, waiting time, and local feeder links all shape how accessible the network feels.
